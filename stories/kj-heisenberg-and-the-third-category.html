KJ Heisenberg and The Third Category

"Excuse me, Ma'am . . . ", Kathy softly knocked on the door of the Director's office as she opened it. "I know you wanted the Africa assignment finished by 5:00PM, but I have a question on this one file . . ." The director looked up from reading the reports on her desk to peer over the rim of her half glasses. Her blue eyes seemed only more intense now framed by her silver hair. "That's quite alright. I can't figure out our objective on this whole East Timor mess anyway," She dropped her highlighting pen in frustration, "What seems to be the snag?" "Well," Kathy moved forward to hand the Director the brown folder, "the Africa assignment needs five GS6s, and this one's a GS10. He is way above this kind of overseas clean-up work, yet he is on the list. From what I can see, he volunteered." "How can you tell that?" the director cocked her head up and peeled her eyeballs down to begin reading the cover of the folder with her bifocals. "The handwriting, ma'am. He wrote his own objectives and perogative assignments. The handwriting on those paragraphs match the script on release forms." Kathy extended a finger to point to the right half of the folder, now splayed on the Director's oak desk. The director blinked, then turned the folder to the inside back page. Finding the 10 digit alphanumeric, she turned to her terminal on the corner of her desk and punched in the code. "Aah. Grey, Jackson K. MB67-594-DJ6. Rated as GS10 with the NSA. Now on detachment with . . . State Department-- huh. What's he doing over at State? What's he doing volunteering for that mess in Africa?" The Director was mumbling, seemingly not realizing she was compromizing security with Kathy there in the room. Actually, she did realize what she was saying, but she was close enough to retirement, and she liked Kathy, so she had begun to 'leak' information around Kathy to ensure that the young woman could replace her when she did leave. The Director took five minutes glancing between the folder and the terminal, as though she were piecing in the blank spots in Jackson's record. "Kathy," the Director now focused at the open dossier, "You should know this one by now. You've sent him out on five assignments like this already." The Director turned the dossier around on the desk and pointed to the initials in the upper right-hand corner on five separate pages. "That is you, isn't it? 'KJ'?" Kathy had never rifled through the whole folder before. She had always checked the top sheet for financial and department clearances and signed them off to their destiny. "Yes ma'am. That is my 'KJ'." "Well," the Director pushed the folder back across the desk to Kathy "If the young Mr. Grey isn't dead yet, who am I to stop him from trying? Send him. --And Kathy-- I think you can start making these calls yourself." "Oh God," Kathy thought to herself, "was that a slam for bothering her?" The warm smile developing on the Director's face told her otherwise. It was no reproach, it was the Director's attempt at foreshadowing, telling Kathy that she'll soon have the job herself. --------------------------- In 1927, Werner Heisenberg enunciated the principle of "Unbestimmtheit", which can be translated as "uncertainty". In general, it can be shown that, if delta-X denotes the effective range in the possible values that might be found by observation for the coordinate X of a given particle, and if delta-P indicates the similar range for momentum, then: dXdP >= h where h is Plank's constant of the Universe. In short, as soon as you measure something, you cannot be sure of it's true position. The way (Dao) that can be spoken of Is not the constant way (Dao); The name that can be named Is not the constant name. ------------------------------ Kathy stared at Jackson's file for a long time. For months, she had been secretly tracking on a yellow pad which files didn't come back from which assignment. She had also been cross-referencing their numbers with this fatality rate-- hoping to find some pattern or odd quirk. Odd numbers? Evens? The ones with double pairs? Sequential? There was no pattern. But she had never felt any guilt playing St. Peter with these files, because she had never known their names . . . they were just files. Now she knew one. What made it worse, this was The Anomaly Folder. She had filed this one in the 'Goat' pile at least four times now, and he kept coming back. Maybe that's why she went and bothered the Director about it-- she wanted to know why this one kept defying her categories. Now she had measured him. Now she had named him. He's not a Sheep-- we were well beyond Sheep at this point (he would never take a pansy assignment). He's not a Goat-- he had come back from too many of those to be a Goat. Kathy stared at the folder on her desk until five minutes before 5:00PM.
